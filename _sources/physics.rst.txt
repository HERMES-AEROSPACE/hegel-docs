Physics and Numerical Methods
=============================

Governing Equations
-------------------

The equations governing the dynamics of any of the fluid models available
in HEGEL may be written in conservation law form:

.. math::

   \frac{\partial \mathbf{U}}{\partial t}
   + \nabla \cdot (\mathbf{F} - \mathbf{D}) = \mathbf{S}

where :math:`t` denotes time, :math:`\mathbf{U}` is the vector of conservative
variables, :math:`\mathbf{S}` the source term vector, and :math:`\mathbf{F}` and
:math:`\mathbf{D}` the generalized inviscid and diffusive flux vectors,
respectively. The entries of these vectors depend on the particular fluid model
being considered.

Space Discretization
--------------------

The spatial discretization is accomplished based on the cell-centered finite
volume (FV) method:

.. math::

   V_{ijk} \frac{d \hat{\mathbf{U}}_{ijk}}{dt} = \mathbf{R}_{ijk}

where :math:`\hat{\mathbf{U}}_{ijk}` are the volume-averaged conservative
variables of cell :math:`(i,j,k)` with volume :math:`V_{ijk}`. The steady
right-hand-side residual is:

.. math::

   \mathbf{R}_{ijk} = V_{ijk} \mathbf{S}_{ijk}
   - \sum_{f \in \mathcal{F}_{ijk}}
   \left( \mathbf{H}^i_f - \mathbf{H}^d_f \right) A_f

where :math:`\mathbf{H}^i_f` and :math:`\mathbf{H}^d_f` represent the inviscid
and diffusive fluxes at face :math:`f` with area :math:`A_f`. The use of the
mid-point rule for evaluating flux integrals makes the method second-order
accurate for multi-dimensional grids.

Inviscid Flux
~~~~~~~~~~~~~

By default, inviscid fluxes are evaluated using upwind-biased flux functions:

- **Roe's approximate Riemann solver**
- **AUSM-family methods** (AUSM+, AUSM+-up)

with reconstruction procedures for high-order accuracy:

- **MUSCL** (Monotone Upstream-centered Schemes for Conservation Laws)
- **WENO** (Weighted Essentially Non-Oscillatory, up to 5th order)

For turbulent and unsteady flow predictions (e.g., NLTE plasma jets), where
upwind dissipation may lead to excessive solution smearing, HEGEL provides a
hybrid upwind-central flux:

.. math::

   \mathbf{H}^i_f = \phi \, \mathbf{H}^{i(\mathrm{up})}_f
   + (1 - \phi) \, \mathbf{H}^{i(\mathrm{c})}_f

where :math:`\phi` is a dynamic sensor activated only in regions of steep
gradients (e.g., shock waves). The central contribution is computed using the
skew-symmetric FV schemes of Ducros et al.

Slope limiters available include van Albada's limiter.

Diffusive Flux
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

Diffusive fluxes are evaluated using Green-Gauss' theorem to determine
face-averaged gradients.

Boundary Conditions
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

Boundary conditions are implemented using ghost or dummy cells. HEGEL
supports:

- **Extrapolation-based** boundary conditions (simple but may produce
  spurious reflections)
- **LODI** (Local One-Dimensional Inviscid) non-reflecting boundary conditions,
  following the ghost-cell formulation of Motheau et al. These minimize
  reflections at boundaries and are critical for unsteady simulations

Additional boundary condition types include isothermal walls, prescribed
inflow/outflow, and periodic boundaries.

Time Integration
----------------

The semi-discrete system of ODEs may be advanced in time using:

**Explicit methods**
   - 3rd-order Strong Stability Preserving (SSP) Runge-Kutta
   - 4th-order classical Runge-Kutta

**Implicit methods**
   - Backward Euler with Data-Parallel Line Relaxation (DPLR), yielding
     block-tridiagonal systems solved along each mesh radial line
   - Convergence to steady-state accelerated via local time-stepping

**Implicit-Explicit (IMEX) methods**
   For problems with stiff source terms (e.g., chemical kinetics) combined
   with non-stiff convective terms.

Grid System
-----------

HEGEL uses point-matched block-structured grids to treat complex geometries
while retaining the efficiency and simplicity of structured grid solvers.

For multi-block grids, parallelization is implemented by splitting the global
MPI communicator into block communicators using ``MPI_Comm_split``. These
communicators are then used to create the parallel layout of each grid block
using PETSc DM objects. Intra-block communication is handled by PETSc
(``DMGlobalToLocalBegin``/``End``), while inter-block communication is managed
by the ``grid_block_int`` class.

Grid adaptation following the procedure of Gnoffo and Hartung is available
for shock-aligned refinement in blunt body simulations.

Solution Strategy for Steady-State Problems
-------------------------------------------

A typical two-step approach for obtaining second-order accurate steady-state
solutions:

1. Compute a first-order solution using a diffusive flux (e.g., van Leer's
   flux vector splitting) with backward Euler and local time-stepping
2. Restart with the second-order scheme (AUSM+ with MUSCL reconstruction
   and van Albada limiter), applying grid adaptation to prevent oscillations
   around the shock
