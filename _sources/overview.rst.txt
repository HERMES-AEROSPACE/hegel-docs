Overview
========

HEGEL (High fidElity tool for maGnEto-gas-dynamics simuLations) is a
high-fidelity and flexible software for hypersonics and plasma simulations.
It is developed by the CHESS group (Center for Hypersonics and Entry Systems
Studies), originally at the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign and
now at the University of California, Irvine, as part of the HERMES-AEROSPACE
project.

HEGEL is written in object-oriented fashion using modern Fortran 2008, and
may be interfaced with other solvers for multi-physics calculations such as
plasma wind tunnel simulations. The governing equations are discretized in
space based on the cell-centered finite volume method. Time integration may
be accomplished using explicit, implicit, or implicit-explicit (IMEX) methods.
Parallelization is realized through the combined use of MPI and PETSc.

Design Philosophy
-----------------

A key design principle of HEGEL is the separation of *physics* from *numerics*.
The solver is designed by minimizing the dependency of the data structure on
the details of the fluid model. In the ideal situation, the solver is only
aware of: number of species, number of temperatures, and number of spatial
dimensions. This allows treating calorically perfect gases, combustible
mixtures, and multi-component plasmas under either equilibrium or
nonequilibrium conditions within the same tool.

For multi-component reactive simulations, the calculation of thermodynamic,
transport, and optical properties — as well as source terms resulting from
collisional and radiative processes — is delegated to an external
physicochemical library. This task is currently accomplished using
`PLATO <https://hermes-aerospace.github.io/plato-docs/>`_ (PLAsmas in
Thermodynamic nOn-equilibrium). Usage of other tools such as EGLIB,
Mutation, Mutation++, Cantera, and KAPPA is also possible with minimal
implementation effort.

Fluid Models
------------

The fluid models available in HEGEL fall into two main categories:

**Calorically perfect gas**
   The working fluid is treated as a calorically perfect gas. Thermodynamic
   properties require only the ratio of specific heats (:math:`\gamma`) and
   the specific gas constant (:math:`R`). Transport properties (e.g., dynamic
   viscosity) are computed based on engineering models such as Sutherland's law.

**Multi-component gas**
   For reactive flows in hypersonics and combustion, HEGEL treats multi-species
   gases as mixtures of dilute gases made of neutral as well as charged
   particles (plasmas). The term *species* may refer to:

   - Chemical components (e.g., N\ :sub:`2`, NO\ :sup:`+`) in multi-temperature models
   - Individual energy levels (e.g., N(i)) in state-to-state approaches
   - Groups of aggregated bound states in coarse-grained reduced-order formulations

   Constitutive relations for thermodynamics, transport, and kinetics in
   either LTE or NLTE conditions follow from the Chapman-Enskog method for
   solving the Boltzmann equation.

The specific fluid model configurations available are:

- **EU_NS** — Euler/Navier-Stokes for calorically perfect gas
- **EU_NS_LTE** — Navier-Stokes with Local Thermodynamic Equilibrium thermochemistry
- **EU_NS_NLTE** — Navier-Stokes with Non-Local Thermodynamic Equilibrium thermochemistry
- **ICP_LTE** — Inductively Coupled Plasma flows with LTE thermochemistry
- **ICP_NLTE** — Inductively Coupled Plasma flows with NLTE thermochemistry

Multi-Physics Coupling
----------------------

HEGEL may be coupled to other solvers to enable multi-physics simulations
using the preCICE open-source coupling library. Compared to a monolithic
approach, this strategy reduces software complexity and maintenance work.

The coupling scheme for a plasma wind tunnel simulation involves:

- **PLATO** — Physicochemical library providing thermodynamic, transport, and
  optical properties as well as collisional-radiative source terms (library linkage)
- **FLUX** — Electromagnetic field solver (MFEM-based FEM) for ICP simulations,
  providing Joule heating and Lorentz force source terms (preCICE volume coupling)
- **MURP** — Radiation transport solver for non-grey media (PETSc-based FV),
  providing radiative source terms (preCICE volume coupling)
- **PATO / CHyPS** — Material response solvers for ablative thermal protection
  systems (preCICE surface coupling)

The ``coupler`` class manages data exchange via preCICE, with child classes
``coupler_surf`` and ``coupler_vol`` providing methods for surface and volume
coupling, respectively.

Demonstrated Applications
-------------------------

HEGEL has been validated and applied to a range of problems:

- 1D channel flow with LODI non-reflecting boundary conditions
- 2D acoustic pulse propagation
- 3D compressible Taylor-Green vortex decay (DNS at Re = 400, M = 2)
- Hypersonic flow past a sphere (calorically perfect gas, with grid adaptation)
- NLTE hypersonic flow past a sphere coupled with radiation (HEGEL + MURP)
- LTE plasma jet with Kelvin-Helmholtz instabilities (hybrid upwind-central scheme)
- Axisymmetric ICP torch discharge (HEGEL + FLUX + MURP, Plasmatron X geometry)
- 3D ICP torch discharge with 13-block structured grid
