Preconditioning

At low Mach numbers, the compressible Euler/Navier-Stokes equations become poorly conditioned: the ratio of the largest to smallest eigenvalues (\(|u+c|/|u|\)) diverges as \(M \to 0\). This causes slow convergence for implicit solvers and excessive numerical dissipation for upwind flux schemes. Preconditioning rescales the eigenvalues to improve both.

Weiss-Smith Preconditioning

Reference

    1. Weiss, W. A. Smith, AIAA J. 33, 2050–2057 (1995).

The Weiss-Smith preconditioner modifies the pseudo-time derivative by replacing the identity with a preconditioning matrix \(\Gamma^{-1}\):

\[\Gamma^{-1} \frac{\partial U}{\partial \tau} + \frac{\partial F}{\partial x} + \frac{\partial G}{\partial y} = S\]

The preconditioning matrix in primitive variables \(Q = (p, u, v, w, T)^T\) is:

\[\begin{split}\Gamma_p = \begin{pmatrix} \theta & 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 \\ \theta u & \rho & 0 & 0 & 0 \\ \theta v & 0 & \rho & 0 & 0 \\ \theta w & 0 & 0 & \rho & 0 \\ \theta H - 1 & \rho u & \rho v & \rho w & \rho c_v \end{pmatrix}\end{split}\]

where the preconditioning parameter is:

\[\theta = \frac{1}{M_{rs}^2 c^2} + \frac{\gamma - 1}{c^2}\]

and the reference Mach number is:

\[M_{rs} = \min\bigl(\max(M, \epsilon_{pc}), 1\bigr)\]

This clamps the reference Mach number between a floor value \(\epsilon_{pc}\) and 1, ensuring that preconditioning is active only at low Mach numbers and smoothly transitions to the unmodified system at \(M \ge 1\).

Modified eigenvalues: Defining \(\alpha = \tfrac{1}{2}(1 - M_{rs}^2)\), the preconditioned wave speeds used in the dissipation term are:

\[\begin{split}V_n' &= (1 - \alpha)\,u_n = \tfrac{1}{2}(1 + M_{rs}^2)\,u_n \\ c' &= \sqrt{\alpha^2 u_n^2 + M_{rs}^2 c^2}\end{split}\]

The preconditioned eigenvalues are \(|V_n' \pm c'|\) for the acoustic waves and \(|u_n|\) for the entropy and shear waves. As \(M \to 0\), all eigenvalues become \(O(u)\), eliminating the disparity between acoustic and convective speeds.

Input Settings

Activation:

Preconditioning is activated by selecting a preconditioned flux scheme:

NUM_FLUX    = ROE_IPC_WS    # Inviscid Preconditioned Roe
FLUID_M_INF = 0.3           # Reference Mach number

or:

NUM_FLUX    = ROE_VPC_WS    # Viscous Preconditioned Roe
FLUID_M_INF = 0.1

Key parameters:

Keyword

Description

FLUID_M_INF

Reference (freestream) Mach number for preconditioning scaling

NUM_FLUX

Must be ROE_IPC_WS or ROE_VPC_WS to activate

Source files:

  • src/fluid_eu_ns/EU_pc.F90 (pc_mat, WSp_pc_mat, WS_pc_mat)

  • src/fluid_eu_ns/EU_nflux_roe_pc.F90 (roe_IPC_WS_flux, roe_VPC_WS_flux)

  • src/fluid_eu_ns/EU_nflux_roe_utils_pc.F90 (roe_WS_diss_term)

Time-step computation: When preconditioning is active, the time step uses the preconditioned eigenvalues (time_step_WS_ipc).

All (CFL based on preconditioned eigenvalues)

Warning

Preconditioning is currently available only for the calorically perfect gas (EU/NS) model with the Roe flux scheme. Multi-species or multi-temperature models do not support preconditioning.

Grid Adaptation

Although not a preconditioning method, HEGEL’s shock-aligned grid adaptation (Gnoffo-Hartung method) is another technique for improving solution quality and is documented here for completeness.

Gnoffo-Hartung Grid Adaptation

Reference

    1. Gnoffo, AIAA Paper 83-1913 (1983).

Redistributes grid points along wall-normal lines to cluster cells near the bow shock and in the boundary layer. Uses B-spline interpolation for smooth redistribution.
